          saas leaders spend a lot of time on the micro. when will this feature ship? is that customer churning? what led to that production issue? even the most senior leaders often get caught here.

then you see okrs to match. trailing churn, grow revenue from new features, maintain x% uptime. and processes and tools spring up to measure all of this with drilldowns and rollups to match.

sure sometimes this starts with a strategic vision, identifying areas of opportunity, and then breaking that down into manageable tasks. planfully creating process and the organization to execute.

but i reckon that's less common than we'd like to admit. the secret is, it's often more like this:

- oh bleep, i just got a call that super important customer threatened to leave!

- let's get five people in an emergency meeting with the customer!

- the customer said the competitor has a better version of a highy specific feature! is an upgrade on our roadmap? how can we rally the team to execute on it sooner? we need a swat effort - who can we pull into a team without making any sacrafices?

- we need a r/y/g to discuss customers with this issue. and other customers with issues. and other issues we've noticed internally. we'll go through the list item by item, thrice weekly at 8 am.

- etc.

and this is of course all common and natural. once the firehouse starts it's hard to fit in anything but more reaction.

so the products and processes adapt to fit reality. and they grow. so we attempt to make that process we don't have time for twice as fast. and the overwhelm sets in, and the to do list gets too long.

since so many very succesful companies experience this that we assume it must be the way.

there isn't a magic fix, but i don't think it happens by saying "i'm going to spend less time on the micro by optimizing the time i spend on the micro." 

it starts by carving out time for macro strategy. this may be the 30 minutes that doesn't feel urgent researching your place in the market. maybe it's a call with a customer that doesn't need anything right now. or - i hope - a solution like rabble ai which can put the strategic priorities right in front of you in plain language.

will this turn the firehouse of micro off? no, nothing will. but at least it prevents the micro from completely washing the strategic away.
