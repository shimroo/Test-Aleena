photo gallery




helicopter observation 
                                        the rosenstiel school’s one-of-a-kind flying scientific laboratory, the helicopter observation platform, or hop, is equipped with state-of-the-art technology to collect vital information about earth’s environmental processes, including for climate and human health studies. 
                                    




the center for southeastern tropical advanced remote sensing (cstars)
                                        launched in 2003, cstars provides the global scientific community with real-time, high-resolution satellite imagery for environmental monitoring of the gulf of mexico, southeastern united states, northern south america, central america and the caribbean basin.
                                    




department of ocean sciences
                                        the ocean sciences (oce) department brings together the diversity of faculty and research programs to understand the ocean as a major earth system.  
                                    




department of atmospheric sciences (atm)
                                        the ocean sciences (oce) department brings together a diversity of faculty and research programs to understand the ocean as a major component of the earth system.
                                    




department of marine biology and ecology (mbe)
                                        the department provides students with the tools, training and opportunities to pursue cross-disciplinary research and develop integrative thinking to expand our understanding of marine organisms and their interrelationships with the environment. 
                                    




department of marine geosciences (mgs)
                                        our educational mission is to provide high-quality undergraduate and graduate education in the classroom, field and laboratory with a goal of producing future geoscientists with the skills and training necessary to increase our understanding of the planet.
                                    




f.g. walton smith
                                        the rosentiel school’s state-of-the-art research catamaran, f.g. walton smith, is specially designed and equipped for research in tropical oceanography. named in honor of the school’s founder, the 96-foot-long vessel’s shallow draft enables it to explore shallow environments areas such as reefs, mangroves, and grassbeds.
                                    




department of environmental science and policy (evr)
                                        the department offers a broad and interdisciplinary academic program that fosters critical thinking across disciplines with technology and innovation at the forefront. students enrolled in the program have the option to pursue a breadth of specializations  related to the cultural intersection between humans and the sea.
